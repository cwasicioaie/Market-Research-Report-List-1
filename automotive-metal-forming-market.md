<p><p>The automotive metal forming market consists of various processes used to shape metal components. Roll forming involves bending metal sheets into desired shapes as they are fed through rollers. Stretch forming stretches metal sheets over a mold for curved shapes. Stamping uses dies to cut or shape metal under pressure. Deep drawing transforms flat sheets into deeper, hollow shapes. Hydroforming uses high-pressure fluid to shape metal, allowing complex designs. Other methods include bending and forging, catering to diverse automotive requirements.</p></p>
