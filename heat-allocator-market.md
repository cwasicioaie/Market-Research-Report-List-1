<p><ul><li>Evaporating Style Heat Allocator</li><li>Electric Heat Allocator</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Heat allocators are devices used to measure and distribute heat in multi-unit buildings. The evaporating style heat allocator operates by using a liquid that evaporates in response to heat, providing a reading based on the amount of heat absorbed. In contrast, electric heat allocators utilize sensors to measure temperature and calculate heat usage, often incorporating digital displays for precise tracking. Both types aim to promote energy efficiency and fair billing by accurately reflecting individual heating consumption in shared spaces.</p></p>
