<p><p>Marine power encompasses various renewable energy sources derived from oceanic conditions. Wave energy harnesses the energy produced by surface waves, converting their movement into electricity. Tidal energy exploits the gravitational pull of the moon and sun, generating power from the rise and fall of tides. Ocean thermal energy utilizes temperature differences between warm surface water and colder deep water to produce energy. Additionally, other marine power market types include salinity gradient energy and thermal gradients, expanding the potential for sustainable ocean-based power generation.</p></p>
